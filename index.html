My Projects & Thesis

⚡ Improvement of Efficiency of Distribution Network with Renewable Energy Resources

Final Year Thesis (PIEAS, June 2026)
Supervised by Dr. Babar Hussain & Co-Supervised by Dr. M. Abubakar

This research tackled technical losses (line resistance, reactive power flow, and voltage drops) in electrical distribution networks in Pakistan. The network was simulated under four scenarios on the IEEE 14-bus test system: uncompensated, with synchronous condensers only, with DFIG wind turbines only, and with a combined DFIG & Synchronous Condenser configuration.

Key Results: The combined configuration achieved the best performance, drastically reducing total real power losses from 21.88 MW to 7.35 MW (a 66.4% reduction), improving system efficiency to 97.24%, and keeping all bus voltages within the safe 0.95 to 1.05 pu range.

📈 THD Analysis of SPWM Inverters

Power Electronics CEP — PIEAS

Comprehensive analysis of Total Harmonic Distortion (THD) in Sinusoidal Pulse Width Modulated (SPWM) inverters using MATLAB/Simulink. Modeled a 350V DC → 220V RMS H-bridge inverter tested at PWM switching frequencies of 5, 7.5, 10, 15 and 20 kHz across modulation indices of 0.1 to 1.5. Identified optimal operating point at Ma = 1.0 and 20 kHz achieving lowest THD of 0.50% — well within IEEE-519 harmonic limits.

🔌 Regulated 12V/0.5A DC Power Supply

Electronic Devices & Circuits (EDC) CEP — PIEAS, June 2023

Designed and implemented a regulated 12V, 0.5A DC power supply from 220V AC mains. The design included a 230V→12V step-down transformer, bridge rectifier (IN4001 diodes), 1500µF smoothing capacitor, and LM7812CV linear voltage regulator. Verified output voltage stability, load regulation, line regulation, and ripple voltage (<50mV) using oscilloscope measurements on both simulated and practical hardware circuits.
