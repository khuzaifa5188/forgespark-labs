⚡ Improvement of Efficiency of Distribution Network with Renewable Energy Resources

Final Year Thesis (PIEAS, June 2026)
Supervised by Dr. Babar Hussain & Co-Supervised by Dr. M. Abubakar

This research tackled technical losses (line resistance, reactive power flow, and voltage drops) in electrical distribution networks in Pakistan. The network was simulated under four scenarios on the IEEE 14-bus test system: uncompensated, with synchronous condensers only, with DFIG wind turbines only, and with a combined DFIG & Synchronous Condenser configuration.

Key Results: The combined configuration achieved the best performance, drastically reducing total real power losses from 21.88 MW to 7.35 MW (a 66.4% reduction), improving system efficiency to 97.24%, and keeping all bus voltages within the safe 0.95 to 1.05 pu range.

❄️ Design & Development of a Cryostat System for HTS Coil Operation

Final Year Project — PIEAS & PTPRI (Sep 2025 – Jun 2026)
Supervised by Dr. Zahoor Ahmad (PTPRI), Dr. Abdul Basit (PIEAS) & Sr. Engr. Saira Gulfam (PTPRI)

Designed and validated a cryogen-free, vacuum-insulated cryostat for operating High-Temperature Superconducting (HTS) REBCO coils at 50 K. The system uses a single-walled SS304 cylindrical shell (Ø304 mm, 600 mm long, 6 mm thick), eliminating conventional double-walled complexity.

Structural Analysis (ANSYS): Static structural and linear buckling simulations under 101.3 kPa external pressure + 50 N cryocooler load. Max stress = 28 MPa at base region (SS304 yield strength: 205 MPa). Circumferential stress: 7.70 MPa; Longitudinal: 3.85 MPa.

Thermal Analysis (ANSYS + COMSOL): Steady-state simulation of radiative heat inleak (253 W, 2.21 m² surface, emissivity 0.25) and conductive inleak (0.088 W via three G10 supports). Design heat load: 278.4 W (incl. 10% parasitic margin). Validated stable 50 K zone cooling via Hex-BN posts on cryocooler cold head.

Analysis & Calculations:

⚡ Green Synthesized Banana Peel Carbon-CoO Electrodes for Supercapacitors

Final Year Project — PIEAS (2025–2026)

Synthesized biomass-derived activated carbon from agricultural waste (banana peels) and engineered a cobalt oxide (CoO) composite electrode for high-performance supercapacitors.

Research & Experimental Steps:

  • Precursor Treatment: Dried, ground, and defatted banana peel biomass (bran) before protein extraction using NaOH and bleaching via H₂O₂ to isolate cellulose.
  • Synthesis: Performed molten-salt synthesis of CoO nanoparticles and nanowires (calcination at 850°C for 2h). Blended carbon to CoO at 2:1 wt%.
  • Fabrication: Coated the active composite slurry on conductive nickel foam substrates using a PVA binder-assisted drop-casting technique.
  • Performance: Analyzed electrochemistry via Cyclic Voltammetry (CV), Galvanostatic Charge-Discharge (GCD), and EIS in 1M KOH. Achieved a peak specific capacitance of **83.78 F/g** (with CoO nanowires), a high power density of **3672 W/kg**, and energy density of **5.1 Wh/kg**.

🚀 Design of a Lightweight Type IV Composite Pressure Vessel (COPV)

Composite Materials CEP — PIEAS (2025)

Engineered a lightweight Type IV Composite Overwrapped Pressure Vessel (COPV) to safely store hydrogen gas at a service pressure of 200 bar (20 MPa) for automotive fuel cell applications.

Design Optimization & Analysis:

  • Materials Selection: Sized a 4.5mm blow-molded High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) gas barrier liner, overwrapped with high-strength Toray T700S-12K carbon fiber in a toughened epoxy (RTM6) matrix.
  • Laminate Architecture: Optimized a hybrid winding pattern (±17° low-angle helical for boss integration + 90° hoop layers for radial pressure containment + ±58° outer helical layers for damage tolerance).
  • Evaluation: Calculated hoop and longitudinal stress profiles. The optimized 11mm laminate shell achieved a predicted burst pressure of **510–540 bar** (safety factor >2.55, complying with ISO 11515 and ECE R134 standards) and a **15–18% weight reduction** over standard helical winding.
